Inspired by [uses.tech](https://uses.tech), here's what I'm actually using day-to-day.

## Editor

**VS Code** with the Catppuccin theme. I keep trying alternatives and keep coming back. The extension ecosystem wins every time.

Extensions I can't live without: ESLint, Prettier, GitLens, and the Astro language tools.

## Terminal

**Ghostty** on macOS. Fast, minimal config, just works. I use zsh with a two-line prompt: directory on top, `$` below. No fancy git status in the prompt — I run `git status` when I want to know.

## Browser

**Firefox** for development. I'll die on this hill. DevTools are better for CSS work and I like supporting a non-Chromium engine.

## Hosting

**Cloudflare Workers** with D1 and R2. Free tier handles everything I throw at it. This site, three side projects, and a URL shortener — total monthly bill: $0.

## The small stuff

- **Raycast** — launcher, clipboard history, window management
- **1Password** — I have 400+ entries and that's fine
- **Figma** — even for quick wireframes, it's faster than pen and paper for me
- **NetNewsWire** — RSS reader, free, open source, native Mac app

## This site

Built with **IndiePub** on Astro. I post notes from my phone, articles from VS Code, and everything syndicates to Bluesky and Mastodon automatically. The IndieWeb dream, basically.
