The city approved a $14 million transit improvement package in January. Two months later, I wanted to know what's been spent, what's been contracted, and what's still in limbo.

I interviewed Council Members Rivera and Okonkwo separately. Their answers were illuminating — not because they disagreed, but because they described two different projects.

## On the bus rapid transit corridor

**Rivera:** "We're on track. The environmental review is done, engineering is 60% complete, and we expect construction bids by Q3."

**Okonkwo:** "The BRT corridor is still in planning. We had to restart the environmental review after the route change in December. Realistically, we're looking at bids in early 2027."

When I pointed out the discrepancy, both offices said they'd "circle back with updated timelines." As of publication, neither has.

## On the bike lane funding

Both agreed that $2.1M of the package was earmarked for protected bike lanes on Morrison and Hawthorne. Both confirmed that zero dollars have been spent.

**Rivera:** "We're waiting on the paving schedule. No point building a bike lane on a street that's about to be torn up."

**Okonkwo:** "The paving schedule was finalized in November. I'm not sure what the holdup is."

## On public input

The city held two public comment sessions on the transit package. Total attendance: 34 people across both sessions.

I asked both council members whether they felt that was adequate input for a $14M allocation.

**Rivera:** "We also accept written comments and have an online form."

**Okonkwo:** "No. Thirty-four people is not community input. But I don't know how to fix that."

## What's next

I've filed a public records request for the engineering contracts and the updated project timeline. I'll update this story when I hear back.
